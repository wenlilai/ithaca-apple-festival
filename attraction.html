Restaurants

Besides apple stalls and food vendors, we do have a variety of restaurants available. The downtownithaca is known for its farm-to-table and fine-dining restaurants, serving fresh and healthy American and ethnic cuisine, which appeal to residents and visitors alike.

Asia Cuisine

126 N. Aurora St.

(607) 256-8818

Monday, Tuesday, Thursday:
11:30am - 8:00pm

Friday-Saturday:
12:00pm - 9:15pm

Sunday:
11:30am - 8:00pm

Located on the corner of Aurora and Seneca Streets, Asia Cuisine is a favorite stop for both locals and the college communities. Asia Cuisine has a reasonably priced array of Japanese, Korean and Chinese dishes and posseses lovely window seats where you can watch the world go by as you dine. Asia Cuisine is an excellent option for vegetarians: their bibim bop and stir-fried kimchi with tofu are delectable.

Collegetown Bagels

301 E. M.L.K. Jr. St., City Centre

(607) 273-2848

Monday-Sunday: 8:00am-3:00pm

It all began with a bagel. Collegetown Bagels, to be exact. For over 20 years, stacks of excellent bagels have formed the foundation of the Brous & Mehaffey stores, now numbering five Ithaca locations. Online ordering and call-ins are available for pickup orders. Make sure that your picking up from the N. Aurora St. location!

Ithaca Ale House

111 N. Aurora St., Restaurant Row

(607) 256-7977

Monday: 11am-9pm

Tuesday: Closed

Wednesday-Thursday: 11am-9pm

Friday-Saturday: 11am-10pm

Sunday: 11am-8pm

Located in the heart of Restaurant Row on Aurora Street, the Ithaca Ale House is an American bar and grill with an extensive, ever changing selection of 20 tap craft beers with brews from all over the country.

A great place to meet for drinks and appetizers, or a full blown dinner with friends, the Ale House offers tasty appetizers, salads and soups, an extensive selection of burgers, and dinner entrees ranging from sandwiches to heaping plates of salad. Try their mini sliders with eggplant fries, Ale House blackened cajun pasta, or Maryland crabcake salad.

Friendly Reminders

Here are a few reminders that you should take note about during the festival! Our community would like everyone to have an enjoyable event while keeping the health and safety of our community and visitors as our top priority !
BE SAFE, BE RESPONSIBLE, ENJOY THE FESTIVAL

  • Due to the covid situation, all attendees and vendors are encouraged to be fully vaccinated.
  • Masks are required to be worn at all times during the festival to keep everyone safe at all times regardless of vaccination status.
  • It is also encouraged to keep a social distancing of 6 feet.
  • Do take note that most of vendors accepts cash, so please use cash when purchasing.
